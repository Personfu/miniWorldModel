## Intro
> 🔗 https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quotient_(universal_algebra)

In [mathematics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematics "Mathematics"), a **quotient algebra** is the result of [partitioning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Partition_of_a_set "Partition of a set") the elements of an [algebraic structure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algebraic_structure "Algebraic structure") using a [congruence relation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Congruence_relation "Congruence relation"). Quotient algebras are also called **factor algebras**. Here, the congruence relation must be an [equivalence relation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Equivalence_relation "Equivalence relation") that is additionally _compatible_ with all the [operations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operation_\(mathematics\) "Operation (mathematics)") of the algebra, in the formal sense described below. Its [equivalence classes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Equivalence_class "Equivalence class") partition the elements of the given algebraic structure. The quotient algebra has these classes as its elements, and the compatibility conditions are used to give the classes an algebraic structure.

The idea of the quotient algebra abstracts into one common notion the quotient structure of [quotient rings](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quotient_ring "Quotient ring") of [ring theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ring_theory "Ring theory"), [quotient groups](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quotient_group "Quotient group") of [group theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Group_theory "Group theory"), the [quotient spaces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quotient_space_\(linear_algebra\) "Quotient space (linear algebra)") of [linear algebra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linear_algebra "Linear algebra") and the [quotient modules](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quotient_module "Quotient module") of [representation theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Representation_theory "Representation theory") into a common framework.
